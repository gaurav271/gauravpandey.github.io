Research Interests

Core Research

Simulation of photoelectron imaging experiments

Developing R-matrix–based theoretical frameworks to describe angle- and energy-resolved photoelectron spectra in strong-field ionization experiments. This work focuses on computing partial scattering cross-sections and continuum electron dynamics in multi-electron systems, enabling direct comparison with time-resolved photoelectron imaging measurements under XUV–IR fields.

Core Research

Coupled Electron–Nuclear Motion

Exploring non-adiabatic dynamics driven by electronic coherences at conical intersections. Special focus on Jahn–Teller systems such as CH₄⁺ and photoinduced processes in norbornadiene. Understanding how vibronic coupling and electronic phase relationships govern ultrafast reaction pathways.

Methodology

Quantum Coherence Control

Designing laser-driven control schemes using ultrashort (1–3 fs) pulses to manipulate electronic coherence. Studying carrier-envelope phase (CEP) effects and coherent superpositions of electronic states to selectively steer chemical reactions and fragmentation channels.

Methodology

Computational Method Development

Developing hybrid quantum–classical frameworks for simulating ultrafast molecular dynamics. Includes trajectory surface hopping (SHARC), quantum projected distribution (QPD) methods for initial condition sampling, and integration of ab initio electronic structure data with time-dependent propagation schemes.
